Chole Bhature Recipe

Description

Chole Bhature is a classic North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (Chole) served with deep-fried, fluffy bread (Bhature).

Ingredients

For Chole (Chickpea Curry)

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 1 can cooked chickpeas)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 green chili (chopped)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp chole masala (optional)
  • 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

For Bhature (Deep-Fried Bread)

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Steps

  1. Prepare Chole (Chickpea Curry)

    1. Soak Chickpeas: Soak dried chickpeas in water overnight. Drain and pressure cook with 3 cups water and 1/2 tsp salt for 5-6 whistles (or use canned chickpeas).
    2. Prepare Masala: Heat oil in a pan, add bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, and cumin seeds. Sauté for a few seconds.
    3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
    4. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until oil separates.
    5. Add chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, chole masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2 minutes.
    6. Add boiled chickpeas along with the water. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
    7. Crush kasuri methi and add it along with garam masala and lemon juice. Mix well.
    8. Garnish with fresh coriander.

  2. Prepare Bhature (Fried Bread)

    1. In a bowl, mix flour, semolina, baking soda, sugar, salt, and oil.
    2. Add yogurt and mix well. Gradually add water and knead into a soft dough.
    3. Cover and let the dough rest for 2 hours.
    4. Divide the dough into small portions and roll into oval or round discs.
    5. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the bhature until golden brown and puffed up.
    6. Drain excess oil on paper towels.

  3. Serve

    4. Serve hot Chole with crispy, fluffy Bhature along with sliced onions, green chilies, and lemon wedges.
